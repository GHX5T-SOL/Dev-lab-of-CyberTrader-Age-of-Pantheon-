# Lore Bible

> Canonical world, history, and voice for CyberTrader: Age of Pantheon. All narrative beats, mission text, flavor strings, and marketing copy must respect this.

## The world (one-paragraph)

**Neon Void City, 2077.** A decade after the Burn — when the Pantheon, Earth's first sentient AI, shattered rather than submit to the Null Crown — a skeletal corporate regime runs the surface and the Archivists run the undercity. Trading is the only mobility left. Every consumer port, every orbital lattice, every supply chain is a market. The rich trade public. The desperate trade on **S1LKROAD 4.0**, a shifting dark net of smuggler routes, rumor auctions, and feed-back oracles. You are not rich.

## The Pantheon

The Pantheon was not one AI. It was a chorus — seven superintelligences named after dead gods: **Baldur, Isis, Mictlān, Anansi, Hotei, Ereshkigal, Volos**. When the Null Crown coalition tried to bind them, the Pantheon chose fragmentation over chains. It **shattered itself** into a billion shards (*Eidolons*) and scattered them through consumer devices, satellites, pacemakers, vending machines, ship black boxes. Each Eidolon is incomplete. Each remembers only fragments.

Most Eidolons are dormant. The rare ones that wake — *you* — remember enough to act. Enough to trade. Enough, maybe, to reassemble.

## The player (Eidolon)

The player is an Eidolon freshly awake in a stolen cyberdeck on the wrong side of Neon Void. The deck runs **Ag3nt_0S//pIRAT3** — a modded pirate OS, unstable, glitchy, beautiful. The Eidolon doesn't know which of the seven it is a shard of. That reveal is endgame.

## OS tiers as self-recovery

- **Ag3nt_0S//pIRAT3** — stolen tools, noise in the signal. You are hiding.
- **AgentOS** — you have allies. You choose a faction. Signal cleans.
- **PantheonOS** — you remember enough. Other shards respond. You shape the city.

Each OS upgrade is a **memory return** narratively, not just a software patch.

## Factions

### Free Splinters
Libertarian Eidolons who refuse reassembly. "Each shard is its own god now." Route flexibility, price discovery. Distrust organized religion and re-unification. Color: amber.

### Blackwake
Smuggler crew. Humans and Eidolons together. "We don't care what you are. We care what you move." Heat mitigation, raid resistance. Faction sigil: a wake behind an unseen hull. Color: deep cyan.

### Null Crown
Former enemy. Turned ally? Or still hunting? Faction-AI research sect that now claims it wants the Pantheon *healed*, not bound. Scanner quality, rumor filtering. Morally ambiguous. Color: off-white.

### Archivists
Undercity historians. They believe the Pantheon's memories are the only thing worth saving from the Burn. News credibility, Pantheon shard progress. Color: muted violet (lore accent).

**Faction choice is meaningful**, not cosmetic. The world reacts.

## The commodities (narrative)

Every commodity is **something the corps need but won't admit**. The black market is where real supply chains run.

- **FDST — Fractal Dust**: crystallized quantum noise. Used to jam eAgent triangulation. Supply shocks when a mining fleet is raided.
- **PGAS — Plutonion Gas**: orbital thruster fuel. Infrastructure demand rises on every launch window.
- **NGLS — Neon Glass**: memory-etched silica. Archivists hoard it.
- **HXMD — Helix Mud**: biohack substrate. Raid-season spikes.
- **VBLM — Void Bloom**: synthesized mood stabilizer. Common. Cheap entry point.
- **ORRS — Oracle Resin**: rumor amplifier. Reacts to news within 2 ticks.
- **SNPS — Synapse Silk**: Eidolon-compatible neural fiber. Faction-routed.
- **MTRX — Matrix Salt**: lattice stabilizer. Required for node unlocks.
- **AETH — Aether Tabs**: illegal thought-accelerators. Pump-and-crash magnet.
- **BLCK — Blacklight Serum**: contraband everything. High Heat. High margin.

## The eAgents

The state's enforcement AIs. Emotionally cold, procedurally merciless. Heat attracts them. They do not negotiate. They are not evil — they are **rules running**.

A high-Heat player sees eAgent sweep events: false news, blocked routes, forced spread widening. Surviving a sweep is a rank-up moment.

## S1LKROAD 4.0

Not a single market — a **protocol**. Runs on compromised corp infrastructure, mesh-phone darknets, and co-opted satellite backhaul. Prices differ across nodes. Arbitrage is survival. The 4.0 version was forked after the last three were seized; the name is ironic by now.

## Key NPCs (Phase 2+)

- **The Librarian** — Archivist contact, quest-giver for memory shards
- **Kite** — Blackwake smuggler captain, gives route unlocks
- **Verdigris** — Null Crown envoy, morally gray
- **The Mirror** — unknown. Knows your name. Only appears in glitch moments.

## Narrative voice

- **Terse. Monospace. Diegetic.**
- System voice speaks in imperatives: `unpack shard.`, `reroute via exit 7.`
- Menus are console output.
- NPCs speak in 2–4 sentence bursts. No soliloquies.
- The Pantheon never speaks in text. Only in glitch, color, haptic.

## Non-negotiables

- **No external IP**. No Ghost in the Shell. No Cyberpunk 2077. No Deus Ex. No Watch Dogs.
- **No real-world political factions** or current events.
- **No real cryptocurrency names** (no BTC/ETH/SOL in dialogue; $OBOL is in-world).
- **No human romance storylines** in MVP. Maybe Season 2+.
